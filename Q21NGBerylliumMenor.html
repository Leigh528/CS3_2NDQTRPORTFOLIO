How Ice Cream Was Made:

Ice cream has existed for way longer than you think! But... have you ever wondered how the Chinese made their ice cream back in the Tang Dynasty? Well according to some records, this was the process they used:



Steps:

1. Heat up a mixture of cow, goat, and buffalo milk.
2. Ferment the mixture until it turns into yogurt.
3. Mix the fermented mixture with flour and let it refrigerate.

And it’s done! Ready to be served to the emperor himself!


But I don’t have goat and buffalo milk! How am I supposed to recreate this at home?


Not to fear dear reader! Our modern method of making ice cream is way easier than you think!


Ingredients needed:

1. Milk
2. Heavy cream
3. Condensed milk
4. Any topping available in your house (e.g., Oreos, wafers)
5. Vanilla extract
6. 2 Plastic zip lock bags (One big and one small)
7. Rock salt
8. Ice


Steps:

1. Whisk the heavy cream until it forms stiff peaks.
2. Whisk together the heavy cream mixture, milk, condensed milk, and vanilla extract.
3. Place the mixture in the small ziplock bag.
4. In the bigger ziplock bag, place the ice and rock salt.
5. Put the small bag inside the big plastic bag.
6. Shake it vigorously for about 5-10 minutes.
7. Once you see it solidify, take out the small ziplock bag and enjoy!

Attached below are common ingredients we have used for ice cream through the years!

Time Period Main Ingredient Type Purpose Notes
Ancient China (200 BC) Milk and rice mixture Dairy & grain Base mixture Frozen with snow to create an early ice cream-like dessert
Ancient Persia (500 BC) Saffron, rose water, and honey Flavoring & sweetener Added flavor and aroma Used in chilled desserts called “faloodeh”
17th Century Europe Cream, sugar, and fruit Dairy & sweetener Main ice cream base First true ice cream recipes appeared in European courts
18th–19th Century America Cream, eggs, vanilla, and sugar Dairy, stabilizer & flavoring Texture and taste improvement Basis of traditional custard-style ice cream
Early 20th Century Artificial flavorings and stabilizers Additives To preserve and enhance flavor Used in mass-produced ice creams
